Psychological evaluation

A psychological evaluation is a assessment process used to measure an individual's mental health, emotional state, and behavior through tests, interviews, and observations.

Mental health screening

Mental health screening is a brief assessment process designed to identify signs of mental health issues, such as anxiety, depression, or stress, often using questionnaires.

Depression screening

Depression screening is a brief evaluation tool used to identify signs of depression, such as persistent sadness, loss of interest, or fatigue, through questionnaires or interviews.

Trauma assessment

Trauma assessment is a structured process to an individual's psychological and emotional response to a traumatic event. It identifies the impact on mental health, coping mechanisms.

Bipolar disorder evaluation

A bipolar disorder evaluation is a comprehensive assessment to diagnose and understand bipolar disorder by behavior, and family history through interviews.

PTSD assessment

A PTSD assessment is a systematic evaluation to identify symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder, such as intrusive thoughts, avoidance, hyperarousal.

Suicide risk assessment

A suicide risk assessment is a critical evaluation to determine an individual's of self-harm or suicide. It involves exploring thoughts, plans, behaviors, mental health history.

Psychodynamic therapy

Psychodynamic therapy is a form of talk therapy that focuses on unconscious thoughts, feelings, and past experiences to understand current behaviors and emotional challenges.

Exposure therapy

Exposure therapy is a psychological treatment that helps individuals confront and reduce anxiety or fear related to specific situations, objects, or thoughts.

Family therapy

Family therapy is a form of counseling that involves treating family members collectively to improve communication, resolve conflicts, and address issues.

Individual psychotherapy

Individual psychotherapy is a therapeutic approach where a person works one-on-one with a mental health professional to address emotional, psychological.

Interpersonal therapy (IPT)

Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) is a structured, time-limited treatment that focuses on improving interpersonal relationships and communication patterns.

Motivational Interviewing

Motivational Interviewing (MI) is a client-centered counseling approach that aims to enhance an individual's motivation to change by exploring.

Gestalt therapy

Gestalt therapy is an experiential form of psychotherapy that focuses on the present moment, personal awareness, and self-acceptance.

Trauma-focused therapy

Trauma-focused therapy is a therapeutic approach designed to help individuals process and heal from the effects of traumatic experiences.
